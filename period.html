💆 Pain Relief Tips
🌡️

Heat Therapy

Apply a warm heating pad or hot water bottle to your lower abdomen for 15–20 minutes to ease cramps.

🧘‍♀️

Gentle Yoga

Child's pose, cobra pose, and butterfly pose help relax pelvic muscles and reduce period pain naturally.

💊

Magnesium & Iron

Magnesium-rich foods (dark chocolate, nuts) reduce cramps. Iron-rich foods replenish blood loss.

🥗 Period Diet Guide

EAT THESE

  • Iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils, red meat)
  • Magnesium sources (dark chocolate, nuts)
  • Omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, flaxseed)
  • Ginger tea for nausea and cramps
  • Bananas (potassium reduces bloating)
  • Turmeric milk (anti-inflammatory)
  • Whole grains and complex carbs
  • Plenty of water and herbal teas

AVOID THESE

  • Salty foods (worsen bloating)
  • Caffeine (increases cramps and anxiety)
  • Alcohol (worsens mood swings)
  • Sugary foods and refined carbs
  • Fried and processed foods
  • Dairy in excess (can worsen cramps)
  • Red meat in large quantities
  • Carbonated drinks
📋 Symptoms Guide
Common Symptoms
  • 🤕 Cramps — uterine contractions, normal
  • 🤯 Headaches — hormonal fluctuations
  • 😣 Acne — hormone-driven breakouts
  • 🫃 Bloating — water retention
  • 😴 Fatigue — blood loss and hormones
  • 🔙 Back pain — referred uterine pain
When to See a Doctor
  • ⚠️ Extremely heavy bleeding (soaking pad hourly)
  • ⚠️ Periods lasting more than 7 days
  • ⚠️ Severe pain not relieved by medication
  • ⚠️ Periods more than 3 months apart
  • ⚠️ Bleeding between periods
  • ⚠️ Sudden changes in cycle pattern
